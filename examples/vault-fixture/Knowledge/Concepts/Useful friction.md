# Useful friction

## Summary
Useful friction is the deliberate insertion of a pause, constraint, or confirmation when speed would make the user more likely to harm their own goal. It is not slowness for its own sake. It protects attention, reversibility, or decision quality.

## Definition and reformulation
Useful friction slows down high-consequence actions while keeping low-consequence actions fluid.

## Intellectual territory

### What supports it
High-risk actions benefit from moments that force awareness.

### Implicit assumptions
The designer can distinguish routine actions from consequential actions.

### Limits and counterexamples
Friction becomes harmful when it appears on frequent, low-risk actions or when users cannot understand why the pause exists.
